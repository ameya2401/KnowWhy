Version: 1.0

Status: Draft

# Purpose

This document defines how KnowWhy will be evaluated as both a software product and an academic research project.

The methodology ensures that the research is reproducible, measurable, and based on objective evaluation rather than subjective opinions.

---

# Research Approach

KnowWhy follows the Design Science Research (DSR) methodology.

The project consists of the following stages:

1. Problem Identification
2. Literature Review
3. System Design
4. Prototype Development
5. Experimental Evaluation
6. Analysis
7. Conclusion

---

# Research Hypothesis

Primary Hypothesis

An AI-powered organizational memory system can reduce the time required to retrieve project knowledge while improving the quality and relevance of retrieved information.

---

# Objectives

The research aims to evaluate:

- Retrieval accuracy
- Search speed
- AI response quality
- User satisfaction
- Productivity improvement

---

# Evaluation Participants

Target participants:

- MCA Students
- Software Developers
- Open Source Contributors
- Small Development Teams

Recommended sample size:

10–30 participants

---

# Experimental Setup

Participants will perform the same tasks using:

Method A

Traditional workflow

(GitHub + Notion + Google Drive)

Method B

KnowWhy

The time and accuracy of each approach will be compared.

---

# Sample Tasks

Participants may be asked to:

- Find why a technical decision was made.
- Locate a previous meeting discussion.
- Identify who implemented a feature.
- Find documentation related to a bug.
- Retrieve information about an old project.

---

# Evaluation Metrics

## Quantitative Metrics

- Average search time
- API response time
- AI response latency
- Retrieval accuracy
- Precision
- Recall
- Number of successful searches

---

## Qualitative Metrics

Collected using questionnaires.

Evaluate:

- Ease of use
- User satisfaction
- Trust in AI responses
- Interface usability
- Overall experience

---

# Success Criteria

KnowWhy will be considered successful if:

- Search time decreases significantly.
- Users complete tasks faster.
- AI responses are accurate.
- Users trust evidence-backed answers.
- Participants prefer KnowWhy over manual searching.

---

# Data Collection

Collect:

- Search queries
- Completion time
- User feedback
- AI confidence scores
- Retrieved sources

Personal or sensitive data should not be stored without consent.

# Threats to Validity

Possible limitations include:

- Small participant pool
- Limited integrations
- AI hallucinations
- Internet dependency
- MVP feature limitations

These limitations should be acknowledged in the final research paper.

---

# Ethical Considerations

- Obtain participant consent.
- Do not collect unnecessary personal information.
- Store evaluation data securely.
- Present results honestly.
- Clearly report limitations.

---

# Expected Outcome

The research should demonstrate whether KnowWhy:

- Improves knowledge retrieval.
- Reduces onboarding effort.
- Provides reliable AI-assisted search.
- Is a practical solution for small software teams.

---

# Summary

This methodology provides a structured process for evaluating KnowWhy through measurable experiments, user studies, and performance metrics, ensuring that the research findings are credible and reproducible.
